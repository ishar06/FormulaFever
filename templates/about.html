🏁 What does “FormulaFever” mean?

“FormulaFever” is a crafted name with two ideas in a single phrase: **“Formula”** stands for the exact science of racing — engineering choices, aerodynamics, tyre chemistry and lap mathematics. **“Fever”** captures the emotional intensity of fans — the passion, excitement and the unpredictable drama of racing. Together it represents ***engineering precision + fan intensity*** — our core vision.

Why this name matters for our product

Everything we build — predictive models, visual analytics, and live dashboards — tries to answer: ***what drives performance?*** The name signals that we combine mathematical reasoning with the thrill of racing. Our goal is to transform raw **telemetry** into actionable **insights**.

⚙️ Features — What FormulaFever Does

A practical tour for the presenter: these are the modules you built and can demo.

Past Race Analysis

We ingest historical **FastF1** data: lap times, sectors, tyres, pit stops and weather. Our data pipeline cleans, aligns and stores runs for model training and interactive graphs.

  • Data cleaning & normalization pipeline
  • Visual charts: lap-time trends, tyre-effect curves
  • Season-level **performance summaries**

Driver Comparison & Improvement

Compare two drivers for any season or track: lap **consistency**, overtakes, pit timing, and improvement curve — presented as charts and a single-page comparison report.

  • **Head-to-head metrics** (W/L ratio)
  • Improvement charts across races
  • Performance delta **heatmaps**

Future Predictions

Trained **ML models** predict finishing order probabilities, lap time windows, and pit-stop strategies. Inputs include driver, starting position, tyre, weather, and track characteristics.

  1. Feature extraction from historical runs
  2. Model types: Random Forest, **Gradient Boost**
  3. Outputs: **win probabilities**, lap-time predictions

Interactive Chatbot

A guided assistant that answers questions like ***“Who is likely to win on a wet short track?”*** or ***“Compare Driver A vs Driver B at Silverstone.”***

  • NLP-based question parsing
  • Model-backed **explainable predictions** (XAI)
  • Integration with user queries and live data

Live Race Stats

We fetch live session data via FastF1 (or fallback to cached snapshots) and surface position, lap, tyre, and pit data instantly. If data is temporarily unavailable we show **graceful messages** and cached best-known values.

  • Live feed + data caching mechanism
  • **Error handling** & graceful degradation
  • “Predicted vs Actual” overlays during races

⏱️ Roadmap — Development Path

The journey of FormulaFever — from raw data to intelligent insights. Each phase refined our vision to combine racing passion with data-driven technology.

🏁 Phase 1 — Past Analysis

We started with analyzing historical F1 race data — focusing on parameters like lap times, track conditions, driver performance, and tire usage. The analyzed data is visualized through interactive graphs displayed directly on our website.

⚙️ Phase 2 — Future Predictions

Leveraging machine learning and the Gemini API, FormulaFever predicts future race outcomes — including driver performance, winning probability, and lap timings — using real-world race data.

🏎️ Phase 3 — Driver Comparison & Improvement

We added a comparative dashboard that allows users to evaluate two drivers side-by-side, tracking their progress and improvement trends across multiple seasons.

🤖 Phase 4 — AI Chatbot Integration

To make FormulaFever interactive, we built a smart AI chatbot that responds to queries about race predictions, stats, and analysis — offering insights instantly through natural conversation.

📈 Phase 5 — Live Race Stats

We're adding a real-time race tracking system using FastF1, bringing dynamic race data updates — including positions, speed, and tire strategy — right onto the FormulaFever dashboard.
